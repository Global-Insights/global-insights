Epigenetic antibodies play a crucial role in understanding gene expression alterations through modifications like DNA methylation and histone modifications, without changing the DNA sequence. This market's expansion is driven by rising research activities, technological advancements, and increasing demand due to the rising prevalence of cancer and the aging population. In the United States alone, 1.6 million new cancer cases were reported in 2020, underscoring the urgent need for more advanced research into epigenetic changes.</p>

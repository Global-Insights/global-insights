Electrical conduits are essential protective tubes that house electrical wires, ensuring safe routing within buildings or structures. They protect wires from external damage and provide the proper passage for wiring systems. Two primary types of electrical conduits are available: rigid and flexible.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Rigid Conduits</strong>: Commonly used outdoors, rigid conduits offer superior protection and structural support for electrical cables, panels, and equipment.</li>
<li><strong>Flexible Conduits</strong>: These are typically used indoors for shorter runs between appliances or fixtures, providing ease in installation.</li>
</ul>
<p>Electrical conduits are made from metallic and non-metallic materials and play a critical role in preventing electrical hazards such as short circuits, fires, and electrical shocks. They help maintain a clean and organized wiring layout, simplifying future repairs or upgrades while ensuring compliance with safety standards.</p>

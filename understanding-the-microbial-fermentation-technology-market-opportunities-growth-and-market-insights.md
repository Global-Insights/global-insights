This technology involves utilizing microorganisms such as bacteria, yeast, or fungi to convert sugars or other compounds into valuable products like biofuels, enzymes, pharmaceuticals, and food items. In industries such as food production, fermentation is essential for the production of yogurt, beer, and bread. Additionally, biotechnology relies on fermentation for creating insulin, plastics, and antibiotics. While fermentation is sustainable, its large-scale production can lead to resource depletion and increased energy consumption. Nevertheless, microbial fermentation remains a crucial process, enabling sectors like bioenergy and healthcare to thrive by customizing the process with specific microorganisms and adjusting environmental conditions.</p>

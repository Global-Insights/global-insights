A solar tracker is a critical device that adjusts the position of solar panels or reflectors to optimize sunlight exposure, thus enhancing their efficiency. These trackers are designed to change positions relative to the sun, ensuring maximum energy capture, regardless of weather conditions. Common types of solar trackers include single-axis and dual-axis trackers, which use motors, actuators, and sensors to align solar panels effectively.</p>
<p>As the adoption of solar energy continues to rise globally, solar trackers are becoming essential for improving energy generation and efficiency. The growing number of solar panel installations and government policies supporting renewable energy projects are key drivers fueling market growth. Solar trackers offer several advantages, including reduced land usage, increased energy output, and lower operational costs, making them beneficial for utility, commercial, and residential sectors.</p>

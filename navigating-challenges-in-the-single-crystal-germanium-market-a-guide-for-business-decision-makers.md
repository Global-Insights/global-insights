<h3><strong>Key Applications of Single Crystal Germanium</strong></h3>
<p>Single crystal germanium is widely used in&nbsp;<strong>infrared (IR) systems</strong>&nbsp;for lenses and windows in the 2-12 &micro;m range, as well as in&nbsp;<strong>semiconductors</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>solar technologies</strong>. Its exceptional physical properties, including&nbsp;<strong>mechanical toughness</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>inertness</strong>, and&nbsp;<strong>relative hardness</strong>, make it ideal for use in challenging environments. These attributes contribute to the material's extensive use across various industries, including&nbsp;<strong>semiconductor devices</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>solar batteries</strong>, and&nbsp;<strong>infrared imagers</strong>.</p>
<h3><strong>Market Drivers and Trends</strong></h3>

<li><strong>Technological Advancements</strong>: The rise of&nbsp;<strong>multi-spectral systems</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>shielding applications</strong>&nbsp;is propelling the need for high-purity germanium.</li>
